By Cristina Furlong and Julia Haslanger | @missmarty70 & @juliajrh | Mar 24, 2015

Motor vehicle collision are the leading cause of injury death in children aged 5 to 9, according to the 2013 Child Fatality Report issued by the NYC Department of Health. From 2002 to 2011, 44 children were killed citywide. Eighty percent of those children were pedestrians.

And the issue isn't just limited to younger children. For kids between 10 and 14, the number killed in car collisions was actually higher: 57 children, and 68% of those were pedestrians.

More recently, 30 children aged 4 to 17 have been killed in traffic violence in New York City since 2012, according to data compiled by Streetsblog, a transportation advocacy news source in New York.
